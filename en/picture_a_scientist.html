Picture a scientist

by Luna Gouraud
published on 10/21/25 at 14h51

Document presentation

Picture a scientist is a documentary released in 2020, lighting up the unfairness that women suffer in science.

HIghlighted Researchers

Nancy Hopkins

Nancy Hopkins, biologist et the MIT, illustrates the undervaluation of the work of female researchers, particularly through a lack of recognition and resources compared to her male colleagues. His experience reveals structural inequalities that are often minimized.

Raychelle Burks

Raychelle Burks, chemist, tells us how she experienced both sexism and racism in her scientific career. Her testimony reminds us that some women must face several forms of discrimination at the same time.

Jane Willenbring

Jane Willenbring, geologist, testifies about the sexual harassment she suffered in the field from a superior. By breaking the silence, she highlights the consequences of this violence and the lack of protection for victims.

An engaged documentary

Thanks to those stories, the documentary makes visible injustices that have long been silenced and questions the idea of ​​a truly egalitarian science. It invites us to rethink scientific culture to create fairer and more inclusive work environments.
